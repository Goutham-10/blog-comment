Book Review: "To Kill a Mockingbird" by Harper Lee

Views: 150

"To Kill a Mockingbird" by Harper Lee is a timeless masterpiece that explores themes of racial injustice, moral growth, and compassion in the American South during the 1930s. Narrated by Scout Finch, a young girl growing up in the fictional town of Maycomb, Alabama, the novel follows her father, Atticus Finch, a lawyer who defends a black man accused of raping a white woman.
